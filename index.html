Research Publication

In our paper, “Comparative Analysis of Psychometric data for assessing mental health testing needs using Machine Learning Models,” we study how supervised ML can decide whether a person should proceed to comprehensive mental health evaluation using psychometric survey responses (PHQ-9 and GAD-7). We compare Logistic Regression, Random Forest, SVM, XGBoost, and LightGBM on large self-report datasets and evaluate them with robust protocols (including cross-validation) to support reliable triage. Results show Logistic Regression achieves near-perfect performance (≈99% accuracy and ROC-AUC ≈1.0) while remaining interpretable, and we use SHAP explanations to make model decisions transparent and clinically meaningful—helping build smarter, efficient digital pre-screening workflows.
